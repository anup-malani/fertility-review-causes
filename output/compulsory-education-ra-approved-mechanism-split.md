# RA-approved compulsory-education papers: mechanism split

## Source and decision rule

These sets are generated from Alexandra's completed strict relevance-review sheet,
`child-labor-laws-and-schooling-strict-focused-ra-review.csv`, by reproducible step 77. A blank RA
decision means approved; `FIX` uses the RA-corrected estimand cell. `EXCLUDE` and `UNSURE_PI` do not
enter either set. Three redundant working-paper/version records are collapsed into their preferred
published study.

## Set 1: decreased economic value of children

File: `compulsory-education-child-economic-value-ra-approved-papers.csv`

- 16 distinct approved papers.
- 10 theory/mechanism papers directly discuss child labor, schooling policy, and fertility.
- 6 empirical quantum papers estimate compulsory-policy effects on completed or desired fertility.

The six empirical papers are labeled `REDUCED_FORM_QUANTUM`, not direct mechanism evidence. Their
titles/abstracts generally identify changes in the prospective parent's own schooling. Full-text
review must show the chain `compulsory education -> less child work -> lower economic value of
children -> fewer completed births` before any is labeled direct support for the original
hypothesis.

## Set 2: decreased teenage pregnancy or births

File: `tempo-effects-birth-postponement-compulsory-schooling-ra-approved-papers.csv`

- 10 distinct approved empirical papers after version deduplication.
- The set covers teenage pregnancy, teenage live birth, teenage motherhood, or age at first birth.
- It is the compulsory-schooling evidence stream nested under `tempo-effects-birth-postponement`.

Geruso and Royer appears in both sets because its abstract explicitly reports both teenage fertility
and completed fertility. That overlap is substantive, not a duplicate error.

## Important interpretation rule

Membership in Set 1 preserves the RA-approved quantum/theory relevance judgment; it does not prove
the decreased-value-of-children mechanism. Membership in Set 2 preserves the RA-approved tempo
judgment; full text must still distinguish pregnancy from live birth and temporary postponement
from a change in completed fertility.
